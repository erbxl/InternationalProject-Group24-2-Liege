The team · 20 April 2026

Reflections — what we learned & teamwork

After three intense days at the HELMo Hackathon, each of us takes away something different. Here are our individual reflections on the experience — what we learned, what surprised us, and what we would carry forward.

Erbol Khaliulin

Howest · Applied Computer Science

The most important thing I took away from this experience was the value of dividing work clearly within a team. When everyone has a defined role and a specific area of responsibility, the whole process becomes significantly smoother, people are not stepping on each other's toes, decisions are made faster, and the final result reflects the combined strengths of each individual rather than a blurred group effort.

Working in a hackathon environment, where time is limited and pressure is high, made this lesson even more apparent. Without clear division of tasks, it is easy to duplicate effort or leave gaps unnoticed. With it, the team moves as a unit, and that, I believe, is the most efficient way to work together.

Christopher Anguelov

Howest · Applied Computer Science

One of the most challenging parts was preparing and delivering our presentation in front of everyone. With limited time to complete all our objectives, the experience showed me how much can be accomplished through effective communication and teamwork. By combining our strengths and working towards the same goal, we successfully completed our project.

What surprised me the most was how quickly strangers could become a strong and united team when working toward a shared goal. Despite differences in culture, language, and working styles, we collaborated effectively and achieved our objectives together. This experience showed me the true value of diversity, cooperation, and teamwork in creating success.
