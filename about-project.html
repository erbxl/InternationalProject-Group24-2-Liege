About the project - our challenge & objective

Due to HELMo's rule of a maximum of three Howest students per team, we split into two separate groups, each tackling a different challenge proposed at the hackathon. Here is an overview of both projects.

Team 1: Crisis Food Stock Management

Christopher · Erbol · Manel

The challenge we took on was proposed around the theme of crisis management and humanitarian logistics. The core problem: in emergency situations, such as natural disasters, conflicts, or large-scale disruptions; the distribution of food resources is often inefficient, poorly tracked, and difficult to coordinate across multiple organisations.

Our objective was to design a solution that would allow relief organisations to better manage and track food stock in real time: knowing what resources are available, where they are located, and how to distribute them effectively to those in need.

Over the course of the hackathon, we developed a concept and prototype focused on simplicity and accessibility, a system that could work even in low-connectivity environments, with a clear interface for operators in the field.
